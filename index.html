About Me

I grew up in Springville and have raised my family here – it has been my home during my entire life. I have spent my career as a newspaperman, first at the Springville Herald and now as the manager of the Daily Herald. I have also run a small sound and lighting business here that I started at age 14.

I love this community, and I am excited for all the changes that continue to happen here. As these changes happen, I believe we all need to be part of ensuring Springville remains a great place to live. That is why I am running for city council: to ensure our actions now provide an excellent quality of life for our children and grandchildren.

Open and Transparent Government

I strongly believe in the government being open and transparent in its actions and making every possible effort to inform the public about the issues. Springville is one of the few communities in Utah Valley that does not broadcast its meetings on social media. If I am elected, I will ensure that this changes so all members of the public can view the actions of their government and be informed on how these actions will affect their lives. Communication between Springville City and its residents is something that needs to change for the better.

West side development

During the past two years, my wife Deanne and I have lived in the west part of Springville and witness on a daily basis the safety and infrastructure needs for this rapidly growing area of our community. As a former Springville City Council member, I have worked with many of this area’s existing stakeholders in the past and would continue working to help this area of the community grow in a responsible way that benefits both business and residents.

Getting Things Done

A trait I learned from my Grandmother Margret Conover was how to get things done. It is one thing to know there is a problem; it is an entirely different thing to come up with a creative solution to that problem. Getting things done is one of my biggest strengths.

For example: During my former term on the Springville City Council, Springville’s 950 West – a potential north-south corridor through the west area of the city – was blocked by a railroad track. Many had been asked for years why this street could not be connected over the tracks. I also began asking this question and kept the problem in front of city staff at all times. The solution came through an unlikely source when I was sitting next to a PR representative from Union Pacific Railroad. I discussed the problem with him, got his buy-in and the railroad crossing was put on the “fast track” and completed within four months.

Some of my accomplishments

2021 Utah County Fair

My business partner Mike Duffin and I were asked to co-direct the 2021 Utah County Fair in March of 2021. We accepted the challenge. Many people thought pulling the fair together in a just few months after it was put on hiatus for a year due to COVID-19 was impossible. We brought it all together and presented one of the most successful fairs to date.

Art City Days

I have been involved with Art City Days for the past 30 years, helping to make it better each and every year. I led the effort to make the Art City Days fireworks show one of the first in Utah Valley to be fired remotely by computer in synchronization with a musical score, all while the music was played in the Arts Park and broadcast over radio for those outside the park. Also, for more than 15 years I have secured the talent for the free Saturday night concert that has become an Art City Days tradition for many local families.

Safe Trick-Or-Treat

I have served on the Springville-Mapleton Chamber of Commerce’s Board of Directors since 1984. With the goal of attracting shoppers to Springville’s historic downtown area, I presented the idea of the Safe Trick-Or-Treat event where local businesses and organizations would provide candy to local children on Halloween. This event grew rapidly, and it now annually attracts thousands of children and their parents to the downtown area.

Chamber Scholarship Golf Tournament

I also started the Chamber of Commerce Scholarship golf tournament 20 years ago that has provided thousands of dollars to local high school students in scholarship funds.

World Folkfest

While I was in my early 20’s, a group of local residents asked me to contribute my expertise in sound and lighting to the creation of a new international folk dance and music festival in Springville. My business continued working with the Folkfest for the next three decades, constantly coming up with new and innovative ways to produce the show as the festival grew from a makeshift stage to the permanent structure at the Arts Park. This included working with city staff to install the professional equipment and systems that now assist in producing all sorts of performance and entertainment events at the park.

Springville City Council Member

I have served on the Springville City Council in the past and have been able to accomplish many things during my terms in office. These accomplishments include:

  • I was instrumental in getting Zions Banks to build its location on 400 South in Springville even after the institution had put a for sale sign on its lot because it could not overcome parking issues with Springville City.
  • I also attracted Cougar Automotive and Utah Marine to locate on property on the west side of the freeway that had been designated a Redevelopment Zone. That area is now booming, with many new businesses locating there, adding to the sales tax base in Springville.
  • Backyard chickens: I was very helpful in making that a reality here in Springville during my last term in office as it had been voted out by the previous council.
  • I was able to accomplish the beautification of Springville’s south Main Street entrance through a grant from the Utah Department of Transportation with the help of then Parks and Recreation Director Allan Curtis.

Creating Effective Relationships

Being in the newspaper business my entire life has given me the opportunity to meet and know other community leaders throughout the state and understand the problems they have faced and the creative solutions they have used to better their own communities. As a member of the Springville City Council, I would encourage this “outside of the box” way of approaching problems and finding solutions to the challenges Springville faces.

Through the newspaper industry, my volunteer efforts and my previous city council experience, I have formed relationships with business and civic leaders throughout the Utah County area, including Provo Mayor Michelle Kaufusi, Spanish Fork Mayor Steve Leifson and members of the Utah County Commission.
